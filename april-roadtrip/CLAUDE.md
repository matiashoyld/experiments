# April RV Road Trip - Lake Tahoe, Yosemite & Sequoia

## Trip Overview
- **Dates:** April 4-11, 2026 (8 days / 7 nights)
- **Starting point:** San Francisco
- **Group:** 2 families — 4 adults, 4 kids (5yo, 3yo, 1yo, 4mo)
- **Vehicles:** 2 RVs (21ft each), rented
- **Route:** SF → Yosemite → Sequoia → SF (Tahoe dropped — all Sierra passes closed in April, backtrack would add 7-9 hrs)

## Critical Constraints

### Tioga Pass is CLOSED
Tioga Pass (Hwy 120 East) does not open until late May. All Sierra passes (Ebbetts, Sonora, Monitor) confirmed closed on Caltrans as of March 3, 2026.

### Sequoia — RV is 21ft, ALL ROADS ACCESSIBLE
At 21ft, both RVs clear the 22ft Generals Highway limit. You can drive directly to Giant Forest, Moro Rock, and use Potwisha campground. Enter via either Hwy 198 (Ash Mountain) or Hwy 180 (Big Stump).

### No Hookups Inside National Parks
Neither Yosemite nor Sequoia have any electric/water/sewer hookups at campgrounds. Plan to be fully self-contained or camp at private RV parks outside the parks.

### April Weather
- Yosemite Valley: milder, 40s-60s°F during the day
- Sequoia Potwisha (2,100ft): warm, 50s-70s°F days
- Sequoia Azalea/Grant Grove (6,500ft): cold, lows in 20s-30s°F
- Carry tire chains — required by law during chain controls

## RV Entry Routes (RV-safe)
- **Into Yosemite:** Hwy 140 (El Portal / Arch Rock entrance) — no tunnels, RV-friendly, lowest elevation
- **Into Sequoia:** Hwy 198 (Ash Mountain entrance) — at 21ft you clear ALL restrictions on Generals Highway
- **Alt Sequoia:** Hwy 180 East from Fresno (Big Stump entrance) — wider road, access to Grant Grove

## Kid-Friendly Activities
- **Yosemite:** Yosemite Falls viewpoint (short walk), Mirror Lake (flat, stroller-friendly), Swinging Bridge meadow, Junior Ranger program, Bridalveil Fall (short paved path)
- **Sequoia:** Walk among giant sequoias (General Sherman trail is paved), Big Trees Trail (flat loop, stroller-OK), Grant Grove, Moro Rock (older kids — steep stairs)

## Essential Packing
- Tire chains for both RVs
- Warm layers, hats, gloves (freezing nights at elevation)
- Extra blankets/sleeping bags for kids
- Propane (fill before leaving SF)
- Full water tanks (no hookups in parks)
- Bear-safe food storage (food must be inside RV with doors/windows closed)
- Stroller with off-road wheels for trail walks

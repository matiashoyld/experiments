## 2. BORDER / PASSPORT / ENTRY REQUIREMENTS

### Passports
- **Every person, including infants, needs a valid US passport** for air travel to Canada. There are no exceptions for babies.
- Apply for infant passports well in advance (processing can take 6-8 weeks; expedited 2-3 weeks).
- Both parents must appear in person for a child's first passport application, or provide notarized consent from the absent parent.

### Visa / ESTA Equivalent
- **US citizens do NOT need a visa or eTA (Electronic Travel Authorization) to enter Canada.** The eTA requirement applies to citizens of other countries, not Americans.
- US citizens can enter Canada visa-free for tourism stays up to 6 months.

### Traveling with Minors
- If any child is traveling without **both** parents, carry a **notarized consent letter** from the absent parent(s). The Canadian Border Services Agency (CBSA) may request this.
- Bring copies of birth certificates for all children as supplementary identification.

### COVID / Health Requirements
- As of 2026, **Canada has no COVID-19 vaccination, testing, or quarantine requirements** for entry. The ArriveCAN app requirement was dropped in 2022. Check the Government of Canada website closer to your travel date for any updates.

### Customs Declaration
- You can complete the CBSA Advance Declaration via the ArriveCAN app (optional but speeds up processing).
- Duty-free allowances apply for goods brought into Canada.

### Car Seat Requirements in Alberta
**Alberta law requires:**
- **Infants (under age 2):** Must be in a **rear-facing car seat** until age 2 or until they reach the car seat's maximum weight/height limit.
- **Children (ages 2-6, under 40 lbs / 18 kg):** Must be in a child safety seat (forward-facing harness recommended).
- **5-year-old:** Requires a forward-facing car seat or booster seat if under 40 lbs.

**Important exemption:** Alberta law technically **exempts rental vehicles leased for less than 14 days** from car seat requirements. However, this is a legal technicality -- **child safety experts strongly recommend always using appropriate car seats.**

**Recommendation:** Bring your own car seats from home (2 infant rear-facing seats + 1 forward-facing/booster for the 5-year-old). Checking car seats on flights is typically free. Renting from agencies costs $10-$15 CAD/day per seat, and availability is not guaranteed for the specific type needed.

## 7. ACTIVITIES & MUST-SEES

### Tier 1: Absolutely Must Do

**Lake Louise**
- Iconic turquoise glacial lake. Walk the flat, stroller-friendly **Lakeshore Trail**.
- **Parking fills by 7-8 AM** in August. Arrive very early or take the shuttle.
- Free to visit (Canada Strong Pass).
- Canoe rental available (~$145 CAD/hour for a 3-person canoe).

**Moraine Lake**
- Arguably the most beautiful lake in the Canadian Rockies. Iconic "Valley of the Ten Peaks" view.
- **No private vehicles allowed.** Must take the **Parks Canada shuttle**.
- Shuttle: **$8 CAD/adult, $4 senior, FREE for ages 17 and under.**
- **Reservations required** -- booking opens **April 15, 2026 at 8 AM MT**. 60% of seats released 2 days before each departure.
- For your group: 9 adults x $8 = $72 CAD ($53 USD). Children free.

**Icefields Parkway Drive (Hwy 93N)**
- One of the world's most scenic drives. Lake Louise to Jasper: 232 km.
- Free to drive (no tolls). Multiple pull-offs and viewpoints.
- Budget a full day. Very limited cell service along the route.
- Key stops: Peyto Lake, Bow Lake, Mistaya Canyon, Columbia Icefield, Athabasca Falls.

**Johnston Canyon**
- Popular canyon hike with catwalks bolted to cliff walls.
- **Lower Falls:** 1.1 km each way, mostly paved, partially stroller-accessible. Allow 1 hour.
- **Upper Falls:** 2.7 km each way, steeper with stairs. **Use baby carriers, not strollers.**
- Free. Go early (before 9 AM) to avoid crowds.

### Tier 2: Highly Recommended

**Banff Gondola (Sulphur Mountain)**
- 8-minute ride to 2,281 m summit. Stunning 360-degree views. Boardwalk at top.
- Prices (CAD, dynamic pricing):
  - Adult (16+): ~$70-$75
  - Youth (6-15): ~$45
  - Child (0-5): **FREE** (ticket still required)
- **Family deal:** First 2 hours of the day, 1 free child per paying adult.
- Group estimate (9 adults): ~$630-$675 CAD ($460-$493 USD).

**Lake Louise Summer Gondola**
- Soar to 2,101 m for views of Lake Louise, Victoria Glacier, and wildlife (grizzly bears often spotted).
- Prices (CAD):
  - Adult (18+): $81
  - Senior (65+): $70
  - Youth (13-17): $45
  - Child (6-12): $23
  - Infant (0-5): $9
- No minimum age. 10% discount for advance online booking.
- Group estimate: ~$729+ CAD ($532 USD).

**Lake Minnewanka Boat Cruise**
- Banff's largest lake. Classic Cruise: ~60 minutes.
- Classic Cruise prices: Adult ~$69 CAD, Child (6-15) ~$45 CAD, Under 5 free.
- **Junior Explorer Cruise** (family-focused with treasure hunt): 1 free child per adult.
- Group estimate: ~$621 CAD ($453 USD).

**Columbia Icefield Adventure (Ice Explorer + Skywalk)**
- Drive onto the Athabasca Glacier in a massive Ice Explorer vehicle. Walk on the glass-floored Skywalk.
- Prices (CAD): Adult ~$109, Youth (6-15) ~$71, Child (0-5) free.
- Located on Icefields Parkway, ~2.5 hrs from Banff. Combine with the Parkway drive.
- Group estimate (9 adults + 5yo free): ~$981 CAD ($716 USD).

**Banff Upper Hot Springs**
- Natural hot mineral pools on Sulphur Mountain. Water: 37-40 C (98-104 F).
- Adult: $17.50 CAD, Child (3-17): $15.25, Under 3: Free, Family: $56.75.
- **Note:** Was closed for renovations; confirm reopening status before visiting.
- Swim diapers required for babies. Available for purchase on-site.
- Group estimate: ~$158 CAD ($115 USD).

### Tier 3: If Time Allows

**Bow Lake & Peyto Lake** (free, on Icefields Parkway)
- Stunning viewpoints. Short walks from parking. Great photo ops.

**Vermilion Lakes (near Banff town)**
- Flat, easy walk along the lake. Beautiful sunrise/sunset spot. Stroller-friendly.

**Cascade Ponds (near Banff)**

**Two Jack Lake**
- Less crowded than Minnewanka. Good for a picnic or easy lakeside stroll.

**Surprise Corner (Banff)**

## 8. PRACTICAL TIPS

### Currency & Money
- Canada uses the **Canadian Dollar (CAD)**.
- Current rate: **1 USD = ~1.37 CAD** (1 CAD = ~$0.73 USD).
- **Credit cards** are accepted virtually everywhere (Visa, Mastercard widely accepted; Amex less so).
- **No need to exchange cash** in advance. ATMs available in Banff and Canmore.
- Your US bank may charge foreign transaction fees (1-3%). Consider a no-foreign-fee credit card (Chase Sapphire, Capital One Venture, etc.).
- **Tipping:** Same as the US. 15-20% at restaurants.

### Cell Phone Service
- **Coverage exists** in Banff town, Canmore, and Lake Louise village (Rogers and Telus networks).
- **Very limited or no service** on hiking trails, backcountry, and much of the Icefields Parkway.
- Major US carriers (Verizon, AT&T, T-Mobile) roam on Canadian networks (Bell, Telus, Rogers).
- **T-Mobile:** Most plans include free Canada roaming. Check your plan.
- **Verizon / AT&T:** Add an international day pass ($10/day) or international plan.
- Download offline maps (Google Maps, Maps.me) before heading into the park.

### Weather in August
- **Daytime highs:** 20-25 C (68-77 F), occasionally up to 30 C (86 F).
- **Nighttime lows:** 5-8 C (41-46 F) -- it gets cold at night!
- **Rain:** Afternoon thunderstorms are common. Brief but can be intense.
- **Daylight:** Very long days -- sunrise ~6:15 AM, sunset ~9:00 PM.
- **Wildfire smoke:** Possible smoke from BC/Alberta wildfires. Monitor air quality via the IQAir or AirNow apps.

### What to Pack
- **Layers:** T-shirts + warm fleece/sweater + lightweight waterproof shell jacket.
- **Long pants and shorts** -- temperatures vary dramatically.
- **Hiking boots/shoes** with ankle support and good traction.
- **Rain jacket and/or packable umbrella.**
- **Warm hat and gloves** for early morning hikes or gondola summits.
- **Sunscreen (SPF 50+)** and **sunglasses** -- UV is intense at altitude.
- **Insect repellent** (mosquitoes in August).
- **Bear spray** -- available for purchase in Banff/Canmore ($30-$50 CAD). Carry in an accessible holster, not in a backpack. Cannot fly with it -- buy locally.
- **Baby carriers** (structured backpack-style for hiking) -- essential for Johnston Canyon and trails.
- **Stroller** (jogging stroller with big wheels preferred for gravel paths).
- **Swim diapers** for babies at hot springs.
- **Binoculars** for wildlife viewing.
- **Reusable water bottles.**
- **Car chargers and portable battery packs** (limited outlets in the wild).

### Wildlife Safety
- **Bears (black bears and grizzlies):** Carry bear spray. Make noise on trails (talk, clap, use bear bells). Travel in groups. Never approach or feed bears. Keep 100 m distance.
- **Elk:** The **most dangerous animal in Banff** (more incidents than bears). Keep 30 m (3 bus lengths) distance. Never get between a cow and calf. Late August begins rutting season -- bull elk are aggressive.
- **Never feed any wildlife.** Fines up to $25,000 CAD.
- Store all food in vehicles, not in strollers or on picnic tables unattended.

### Moraine Lake Shuttle Reservation Strategy
- Booking opens **April 15, 2026 at 8:00 AM Mountain Time**.
- Create your Parks Canada reservation account NOW at reservation.pc.gc.ca.
- 40% of seats released on April 15. **60% released 2 days before each shuttle date** (rolling release).
- If you miss the April 15 release, check 2 days before your desired visit.
- Book earliest morning slots for best light and fewer crowds.
- Children 17 and under are free but still need a reservation.

## 10. NEARBY ATTRACTIONS / DAY TRIPS

### Jasper National Park
- 3.5 hrs from Banff via the Icefields Parkway.
- **Status in 2026:** Open and recovering well from the 2024 wildfire. Major attractions are operational including Columbia Icefield, Athabasca Falls, Maligne Lake, Maligne Canyon, and Miette Hot Springs.
- Best as a **long day trip** or overnight. The Icefields Parkway drive is the main attraction.
- Fewer crowds than Banff. Also covered by Canada Strong Pass (free entry).

### Kootenay National Park & Radium Hot Springs
- 1.5 hrs from Banff via Highway 93 South.
- Scenic drive through Kootenay with stops at **Marble Canyon** (short walk) and **Paint Pots** (short walk, stroller-friendly).
- **Radium Hot Springs pools:** Family-friendly facility with a hot pool and cooler swimming pool. Ramps for easy access. Life jackets available for children.
- Entry is first-come, first-served (no advance tickets). Covered by Canada Strong Pass for park entry.

### Yoho National Park
- 30 min west of Lake Louise (just across the BC border).
- **Takakkaw Falls:** One of Canada's tallest waterfalls. Short 1 km round-trip walk from parking (stroller possible). Very impressive in August with glacial meltwater.
- **Natural Bridge:** Quick stop to see the Kicking Horse River flowing through a rock formation.
- Covered by Canada Strong Pass (free entry).

### Lake Louise Summer Gondola
- Sightseeing gondola at the Lake Louise Ski Resort. Excellent for families.
- Great chance to spot **grizzly bears** from the gondola.
- Hiking trails at the summit. Wildlife Interpretive Centre at the top.
- See pricing in Activities section above.

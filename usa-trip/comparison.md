## Pros & Cons

### Seattle + Pacific NW Parks
**Pros:**
- Cheapest option by far (~$5-7K less than alternatives)
- Cheapest flights (SFO-SEA is a short, competitive route)
- Best variety: city + rainforest + mountains + beaches + hot springs
- August is Seattle's BEST month (75-80F, barely any rain)
- Great food scene in Seattle (Pike Place Market!)
- Most diverse itinerary (urban + nature)
- Wildflower peak at Mt Rainier in early August
- Hoh Rain Forest is a once-in-a-lifetime experience
- No passport needed for anyone

**Cons:**
- Most driving/moving between locations (4 different Airbnbs)
- No single "iconic" mega-park like Yellowstone or Banff
- Olympic Peninsula requires long drives between attractions
- Less wildlife than Yellowstone or Banff
- Packing/unpacking with babies multiple times

### Yellowstone + Grand Teton
**Pros:**
- THE iconic American national park - bucket list destination
- Best wildlife viewing (bison herds, wolves, grizzlies, elk)
- Unique geothermal features found nowhere else on Earth
- Single base camp possible (West Yellowstone for all 10 nights)
- Grand Teton + Jackson Hole add great variety
- Most boardwalk-accessible attractions (great with strollers)
- Less driving than Seattle option (one base, day trips)
- No passport needed

**Cons:**
- Mid-price option (~$20,500)
- More expensive flights (SFO to Bozeman)
- August is VERY crowded (peak season traffic jams)
- Altitude (7,500+ ft) - needs acclimatization, watch babies
- Limited cell service
- Car rentals are expensive in Bozeman
- Long distances within the park (Grand Loop = 142 miles)
- Fewer restaurant/town options than Seattle or Banff

### Banff (Canada)
**Pros:**
- Arguably the most scenic destination (turquoise lakes, dramatic peaks)
- FREE park entry (Canada Strong Pass covers Aug 3-13, 2026)
- Can also visit Jasper, Kootenay, and Yoho national parks
- Charming towns (Banff, Canmore) with great restaurants and shops
- Icefields Parkway is one of world's most scenic drives
- Gondola rides with stunning views
- Single base camp (Canmore for all 10 nights)
- Direct flights from SFO (only 2h15m)
- Hot springs, boat cruises, glacier walks

**Cons:**
- Most expensive option (~$22,450)
- EVERYONE including babies needs a US passport for Canada
- Canadian prices are 15-25% higher than US
- Currency exchange adds complexity
- Moraine Lake requires shuttle reservation (competitive booking)
- August is extreme peak season (Lake Louise parking fills by 7am)
- Phone roaming charges (~$10/day per line)
- Bear safety is a bigger concern (grizzlies more common)
- Accommodation is very expensive in peak August

## Recommended Choice

### If budget is the priority: **Seattle + Pacific NW Parks**
At $1,739/adult, it's $500-750 less per person than the alternatives. You get incredible variety (city life, rainforests, volcanic mountains, ocean beaches, hot springs), the best food scene, and August weather that's hard to beat. The downside is more moving between locations.

### If "once in a lifetime" factor matters most: **Yellowstone**
At $2,278/adult, it's the sweet spot. There's nothing like watching Old Faithful erupt, seeing bison herds in Lamar Valley, or standing above the Grand Canyon of the Yellowstone. It's the most iconic American park for a reason, and the single-base-camp approach works great with babies.

### If you want the most stunning scenery: **Banff**
At $2,495/adult, it's the premium option but delivers jaw-dropping beauty. Lake Louise and Moraine Lake are postcard-perfect, the Icefields Parkway is a world-class drive, and the gondola rides offer incredible panoramas. The free park entry helps offset higher Canadian costs.

## Key Action Items (Regardless of Choice)

1. **NOW**: Book accommodation (August books out fast for large groups)
2. **NOW**: Book flights (prices only go up from here)
3. **NOW**: If considering Banff, verify all family members have valid US passports
4. **MARCH**: Reserve rental cars (full-size SUVs sell out)
5. **APRIL 15**: If Banff, book Moraine Lake shuttle tickets (8am MT, sells out fast)
6. **JUNE**: Book activity tickets (gondolas, tours, etc.)
7. **BEFORE TRIP**: Download offline maps, buy bear spray locally, pack layers

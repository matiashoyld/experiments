## 1. Trip Overview & Suggested Itinerary

This trip combines urban Seattle with two spectacular national parks: Mount Rainier and Olympic National Park. North Cascades is discussed but recommended as an optional add-on due to logistics with babies.

### Suggested Day-by-Day Itinerary

| Day | Date | Location | Highlights |
|-----|------|----------|------------|
| **Day 1** | Aug 3 (Mon) | Fly SFO to SEA, arrive Seattle | Pick up rental SUVs, settle into Seattle Airbnb, evening Pike Place Market stroll |
| **Day 2** | Aug 4 (Tue) | Seattle | Pike Place Market morning, Space Needle + Chihuly Garden combo, waterfront walk |
| **Day 3** | Aug 5 (Wed) | Seattle | Museum of Pop Culture, ferry ride to Bainbridge Island (afternoon), Seattle dinner |
| **Day 4** | Aug 6 (Thu) | Drive to Mount Rainier | ~2 hr drive to Ashford. Afternoon: explore Longmire area, check into Rainier Airbnb |
| **Day 5** | Aug 7 (Fri) | Mount Rainier - Paradise | Full day at Paradise: Myrtle Falls trail, Nisqually Vista, wildflower meadows, visitor center |
| **Day 6** | Aug 8 (Sat) | Mount Rainier - Sunrise | Morning at Sunrise area (stunning views), afternoon return. Optional: Grove of the Patriarchs |
| **Day 7** | Aug 9 (Sun) | Drive to Olympic Peninsula | ~4-5 hr drive via Tacoma/Olympia. Stop in Port Townsend or Sequim. Check into Olympic Airbnb (Port Angeles area) |
| **Day 8** | Aug 10 (Mon) | Olympic NP - Hurricane Ridge & Hot Springs | Morning: Hurricane Ridge (stroller-friendly paved trail). Afternoon: Sol Duc Hot Springs (baby-friendly pools) |
| **Day 9** | Aug 11 (Tue) | Olympic NP - Hoh & Beaches | Morning: Hoh Rain Forest (Hall of Mosses, 0.8 mi). Afternoon: Ruby Beach & tide pools |
| **Day 10** | Aug 12 (Wed) | Olympic Peninsula & return | Morning: Lake Crescent (short walk, scenic views). Drive back to Seattle (~3 hrs). Farewell dinner |
| **Day 11** | Aug 13 (Thu) | Fly home | Return rental cars at SEA, fly SEA to SFO |

**Why this order works:** Mount Rainier is south of Seattle, and you can then loop west/northwest to Olympic. This avoids backtracking and keeps driving efficient.

**North Cascades:** While it has no entry fee and some easy trails (Rainy Lake is paved and stroller-friendly), it adds 2.5-3 hours of driving each way from Seattle and would require sacrificing time at the other parks. With two babies, it is not recommended for this trip unless you have high energy and want to swap one Rainier or Olympic day for a North Cascades day trip.

## 2. Flights from SFO

### Round-Trip Flights: SFO to SEA

**Typical Pricing (August):**
- **Budget range:** $150-$250 round-trip per person
- **Average:** ~$200 round-trip per adult
- **Airlines serving this route:** Alaska Airlines, United, Delta, Southwest, Frontier, Spirit

**Budget Airline Options:**
- **Alaska Airlines:** Often the best value on this route; fares from ~$100-180 RT
- **Southwest:** Fares from ~$104 RT; includes 2 free checked bags (great for family travel with car seats and baby gear)
- **Frontier:** Fares from ~$86 one-way, but add-on fees for bags make it less competitive for families

**Children's Fares:**
- Infants under 2: FREE as lap infants (no seat needed) on domestic flights
- The 5-year-old requires a paid ticket at full fare
- You'll need tickets for: 9 adults + 1 child (5yo) = **10 paid tickets**; 2 infants fly free as lap babies

**Flight Duration:** 2 hours 15 minutes direct

### Could They Drive Instead?

- **Distance:** 808 miles, ~12-13 hours nonstop
- **Realistic with stops:** 14-16 hours or 2 driving days
- **Gas cost (2 SUVs):** ~$350-400 total (round trip ~$700-800)
- **Verdict: NOT recommended.** With 2 babies and a 5-year-old, a 12+ hour drive each way is grueling. Flying saves 2 full days of travel time. The ~$2,000 in flight costs is well worth the time saved. If the family wanted to do a scenic road trip, they'd need at minimum 2 extra days each way, which the 10-night timeline doesn't allow.

### Car Seat Requirements (Washington State Law)

Washington state has strict car seat laws:

| Child | Age | Requirement |
|-------|-----|-------------|
| **Infant 1** | Under 1 | Rear-facing infant car seat (REQUIRED until age 2) |
| **Infant 2** | Under 1 | Rear-facing infant car seat (REQUIRED until age 2) |
| **5-year-old** | 5 | Forward-facing harness car seat OR booster seat (required until 4'9" tall, typically age 8-12) |

**Additional rules:** Children under 13 must ride in the rear seat where practical.

### Car Seat Solutions

**Option A - Bring your own:** Most practical if you already own them. Airlines allow car seats as free checked items.

**Option B - Rent from car rental company:** Avis, Enterprise, Budget all rent car seats for ~$10-15/day each. For 10 days with 3 seats = $300-$450 total.

**Option C - Third-party rental services in Seattle:**
- **BabyQuip** (babyquip.com) - Can deliver car seats to the airport. ~$8-15/day per seat.
- **Traveling Baby** (travelingbaby.com) - Airport pickup available.
- **Babies Getaway** - SeaTac airport delivery.

**Recommendation:** Bring your own car seats if possible (airlines check them free). Otherwise, BabyQuip delivery to the airport is the most convenient option.

**Car Seat Cost Estimate:** $0 (bring your own) to $350 (rent all 3 for 10 days)

## 5. National Park Entry Fees

### Individual Park Fees

| Park | Vehicle Fee (7 days) | Notes |
|------|---------------------|-------|
| Mount Rainier | $30/vehicle | No timed entry required for 2026! |
| Olympic | $30/vehicle | No timed entry required |
| North Cascades | **FREE** | Only $5 trailhead parking at select spots |

With 2 vehicles:
- Mount Rainier: 2 x $30 = $60
- Olympic: 2 x $30 = $60
- Total without pass: $120

### America the Beautiful Annual Pass

- **Cost:** $80 per pass (for US residents)
- **Coverage:** Covers driver + all passengers in one vehicle at ALL federal recreation sites for 12 months
- **You need:** 2 passes (one per vehicle) = **$160**

**Is the pass worth it?** At $160 for 2 passes vs. $120 for individual park entries, the pass is only $40 more and covers you if you stop at ANY other federal site (national forests, monuments, BLM lands). It also covers admission for a full year. **Verdict: Buy 2 America the Beautiful passes.** It's barely more expensive and gives much more flexibility.

### Park Fee Estimate: **$160** (2 America the Beautiful passes)

**Important 2026 changes:**
- Parks are now **cashless** -- bring credit/debit cards
- Nonresident (non-US citizen) pass is $250 instead of $80
- Children 15 and under enter free at all national parks

## 9. Practical Tips

### Weather in August

Seattle and the Pacific Northwest are at their absolute BEST in August. This is the region's golden month.

| Location | Avg High | Avg Low | Rain Days | Notes |
|----------|----------|---------|-----------|-------|
| Seattle | 79F (26C) | 57F (14C) | 2-4 | Sunny, gorgeous. Best month of the year |
| Mt Rainier (Paradise) | 60-65F (16-18C) | 40-45F (4-7C) | 3-5 | Significantly cooler at elevation |
| Olympic (lowlands) | 70-75F (21-24C) | 50F (10C) | 2-4 | Warm and dry for Olympic |
| Hoh Rain Forest | 65-70F (18-21C) | 48F (9C) | 3-5 | Always damp/cool under canopy |
| Olympic beaches | 60-65F (16-18C) | 50F (10C) | 2-3 | Cool, windy. Water is COLD |

### What to Pack

**For everyone:**
- Layers! Temperatures vary hugely (80F in Seattle, 45F at Paradise)
- Rain jacket (light, just in case -- it rarely rains in August but the mountains can surprise you)
- Comfortable hiking shoes/trail shoes
- Sunscreen and sunglasses (long sunny days, UV is strong)
- Refillable water bottles
- Binoculars (for wildlife)

**For the babies:**
- Baby carriers (front or back carriers -- ESSENTIAL for park trails)
- Warm fleece layers for mountain elevations
- Sun hats and baby sunscreen
- Portable white noise machine (Airbnb sleep)
- Car window shades for SUVs
- Diapers and wipes in quantity (limited stores near parks)
- Portable changing pad

**For the 5-year-old:**
- Comfortable walking shoes
- Junior Ranger booklet (free at park visitor centers -- great for engagement!)
- Small backpack with snacks and water
- Bug spray
- Binoculars (kids love them)

### Wildlife to Watch For

- **Black bears** -- possible at all parks. Make noise on trails, keep food stored
- **Marmots** -- common at Paradise, Sunrise. The 5yo will love them!
- **Mountain goats** -- Hurricane Ridge, Mount Rainier high elevations
- **Roosevelt elk** -- Hoh Rain Forest, Olympic beaches
- **Bald eagles** -- everywhere, especially near water
- **Sea otters & seals** -- Olympic coast beaches
- **Tide pool creatures** -- starfish, anemones, crabs at Ruby Beach

### Ferry Tips for Olympic

**Getting to Olympic Peninsula from Seattle:**
- **Option A: Drive around** via Tacoma/Olympia on I-5 and US-101 (~3 hours). Most straightforward.
- **Option B: Bainbridge Island Ferry** from downtown Seattle to Bainbridge, then drive to Port Angeles (~3-3.5 hours total). More scenic.
- **Option C: Edmonds-Kingston Ferry** north of Seattle, then drive to Port Angeles (~2.5-3 hours total).

**Ferry details (Bainbridge):**
- No reservations possible -- first come, first served
- Cost: ~$15.60/vehicle + driver one way, passengers ~$11 RT
- Runs every 30-60 minutes
- 35-minute crossing
- **For 2 SUVs:** ~$62 round trip for vehicles + $99 for adult passengers = ~$161 total

**Recommendation for this trip:** Since you're driving from Mount Rainier to Olympic, you'll likely take the land route (I-5 and US-101), which avoids ferry logistics. Save the ferry ride for a fun Bainbridge Island day trip while based in Seattle.

### Other Practical Notes

- **Altitude:** Paradise is at 5,400 ft. Babies and small children should stay hydrated. Watch for signs of altitude discomfort.
- **Cell service:** Very limited to none inside national parks and on much of the Olympic Peninsula. Download offline maps (Google Maps or AllTrails) before you go.
- **National park cashless:** Both Mount Rainier and Olympic are cashless entry. Have cards ready.
- **Bug spray:** Mosquitoes can be present near water, especially at Hoh Rain Forest.
- **Park visitor hours:** No gates close at night in summer at either park, but services/facilities close. Paradise area facilities typically open 10am-6pm or similar.

### Money-Saving Tips

1. **Book flights early** -- set Google Flights alerts now for August 3 and August 13
2. **Southwest Airlines** -- 2 free checked bags per person (huge savings with baby gear and car seats)
3. **Cook most meals in Airbnbs** -- a Seattle Costco run on Day 1 saves hundreds
4. **America the Beautiful Pass** -- $80/pass covers everything for the year
5. **Seattle CityPASS** -- saves up to 50% vs. individual tickets
6. **Book Airbnbs NOW** -- large properties near national parks book out months in advance for August
7. **Bring your own car seats** -- airlines check them free, saves $300+ in rental fees
8. **Pack lunches** for park days -- food options inside parks are limited and overpriced
9. **Gas up in cities** -- fuel is cheaper in Seattle than in small mountain towns
10. **Weekday advantage** -- visiting parks on weekdays (your itinerary already does this) means fewer crowds

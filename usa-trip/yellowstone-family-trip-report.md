### Airport Options Comparison

### Recommendation: Bozeman (BZN) -- Best Balance of Price and Convenience

**Why Bozeman wins:**
- Largest airport near Yellowstone with the most flight options and competitive pricing
- Nonstop flights available on United and Alaska Airlines (~2h 22m flight)
- Only 91 miles / 1h 45m drive to West Yellowstone (the most popular gateway town)
- Best rental car availability and pricing compared to smaller airports
- Costco and major grocery stores (Albertsons, Safeway) on the route for stocking up

**Jackson Hole (JAC)** is the most scenic option and closest to the park, but flights cost $100-$200+ more per person. For 12 passengers, that premium adds $1,200-$2,400 to the trip.

**Salt Lake City (SLC)** has the cheapest flights ($150-$300 RT) but requires a 5+ hour drive each way, which is brutal with babies.

**Idaho Falls (IDA)** has no nonstop flights from SFO, adding layover hassle for the whole group.

### Estimated Flight Cost (Bozeman)
- **Per person round-trip in August: ~$400-$500** (peak summer pricing)
- Babies under 2: Fly as lap infants for free on domestic flights (2 babies = $0)
- 5-year-old: Requires own seat at full fare
- **Passengers needing tickets: 10** (9 adults + 1 child; 2 lap infants free)
- **Total flights estimate: $4,000-$5,000**

## 3. ACCOMMODATION: AIRBNB / VACATION RENTAL

### Best Gateway Towns (Ranked)

**1. West Yellowstone, MT (Top Recommendation)**
- Closest gateway to park (West Entrance, 1 mile)
- Most restaurants, grocery stores, and amenities
- Largest selection of vacation rentals
- Central location for Lower Loop attractions (Old Faithful, Grand Prismatic, etc.)
- Drawback: Most touristy, prices higher in peak season

**2. Gardiner, MT**
- North Entrance to park
- Charming small town, less crowded
- Good for Upper Loop (Mammoth, Lamar Valley)
- Fewer rental options for large groups

**3. Island Park / Henry's Lake, ID**
- 30-45 min from West Entrance
- Often significantly cheaper than West Yellowstone
- More secluded, cabin-style properties
- Less dining/shopping infrastructure

**4. Jackson, WY**
- Upscale resort town with excellent dining and shopping
- 1.5-2 hours from Yellowstone's main attractions
- More expensive accommodations
- Great if also spending significant time in Grand Teton

### Accommodation Strategy

**Option A: One Large House (6+ bedrooms)**
- Properties sleeping 12-16 exist in West Yellowstone area
- Example: Gibbon Meadows Lodge -- 6 bedrooms, 6.5 bathrooms, sleeps 14
- August peak pricing: **$500-$900/night** for large homes
- 10 nights: **$5,000-$9,000**
- Advantages: Everyone together, shared meals, one kitchen to stock

**Option B: Two Smaller Houses (3-4 bedrooms each)**
- More availability, easier to find
- 3-4 bedroom homes sleeping 6-8: **$250-$500/night each**
- 10 nights x 2 houses: **$5,000-$10,000**
- Advantages: More privacy, adults with babies can have quiet space
- Disadvantage: Two kitchens to stock, group split

### Recommendation
Book **one large house** if you can find it -- they book 6-12 months in advance for August. Start searching NOW on Airbnb and VRBO with filters for 12+ guests, West Yellowstone area, August 3-13 dates. If no large homes available, book two 3-4 bedroom homes ideally on the same street or complex.

### Estimated Accommodation Cost
- **Budget estimate: $6,000-$8,000 for 10 nights** (one large house or two medium)
- Cleaning fees: $200-$400 additional
- Airbnb service fees: ~14% additional
- **Total accommodation estimate: $7,000-$9,500**

## 4. PARK ENTRY FEES

### Yellowstone National Park
- **Private vehicle (7-day pass): $35 per vehicle**
- You have 2 vehicles = $70 total
- Annual Yellowstone Pass: $70/vehicle (same as 2 seven-day passes)
- Children 15 and under: FREE

### Grand Teton National Park
- **Private vehicle (7-day pass): $35 per vehicle**
- 2 vehicles = $70 total
- Note: If entering Yellowstone via the South Entrance, you pass through Grand Teton and need to pay both fees

### America the Beautiful Annual Pass
- **$80 per pass** (covers the pass holder + all passengers in a single vehicle)
- Covers ALL national parks and federal recreation areas for 12 months
- **You need 2 passes** (one per vehicle): $160 total
- This covers both Yellowstone AND Grand Teton

### Recommendation
**Buy 2 America the Beautiful passes ($160 total).** This is cheaper than buying separate 7-day passes for both parks ($140) if you consider the flexibility, and it gives you future national park access for a year.

### Estimated Park Entry Cost: $160

## 6. ACTIVITIES & MUST-SEES

### Day-by-Day Suggested Itinerary Framework

**Days 1-2: Arrival + West Yellowstone Settling In**
- Fly into Bozeman, pick up rental cars
- Stop at Costco and Albertsons for groceries
- Drive to West Yellowstone (1h 45m), check into accommodation
- Explore West Yellowstone town, easy first evening

**Days 3-4: Lower Loop -- Geyser Country**

Must-sees (all stroller-friendly boardwalks):
- **Old Faithful** -- Erupts every ~90 minutes; check Visitor Center for predicted times. Benches around the geyser for comfortable viewing. The Visitor Education Center has excellent interactive exhibits for the 5-year-old.
- **Upper Geyser Basin** -- Flat boardwalk loop past Morning Glory Pool, Grand Geyser, Castle Geyser. 3-mile total but can do shorter sections.
- **Grand Prismatic Spring** -- 0.8-mile flat wooden boardwalk. Stunning rainbow-colored hot spring, the largest in North America. Go early morning or late afternoon for better parking and fewer crowds.
- **Fountain Paint Pots** -- 0.5-mile boardwalk loop, 13 different geothermal features. Kids love the bubbling mud pots.
- **Midway Geyser Basin** -- Short boardwalk to see Grand Prismatic from basin level.
- **Kepler Cascades** -- Easy 2-mile round-trip along Firehole River to waterfalls.
- **Biscuit Basin** -- Short boardwalk, usually less crowded than Upper Geyser Basin.

**Day 5: Lower Loop -- Canyon Country**
- **Grand Canyon of the Yellowstone** -- Multiple viewpoints with minimal walking. Artist Point is the most famous view. Uncle Tom's Trail is 328 steps down (adults only, take turns with babies).
- **Upper and Lower Falls** -- Spectacular waterfalls visible from overlooks.
- **Hayden Valley** -- Drive-through wildlife viewing (bison, elk, sometimes bears). Best at dawn/dusk.

**Day 6: Upper Loop -- Mammoth & Lamar**
- **Mammoth Hot Springs** -- Travertine terraces on boardwalks (Upper and Lower Terraces, 1.5-2 miles total). Stroller-accessible on Lower Terrace. Elk often graze on the lawns right in the village.
- **Lamar Valley** -- "America's Serengeti." Best wildlife viewing in the park. Drive through at sunrise or sunset to spot wolves, bison herds, grizzly bears, pronghorn, coyotes. Bring binoculars. Completely car-based activity -- perfect with babies.
- **Tower Fall** -- Short walk to overlook of 132-foot waterfall.

**Day 7: Grand Teton National Park Day**
- Drive south through Yellowstone to Grand Teton (about 1.5-2 hours from West Yellowstone)
- **Jenny Lake Scenic Cruise** -- 1-hour narrated boat tour. Adults $30, children 2-11 $25. Stunning Teton views.
- **Jenny Lake Shuttle + Hidden Falls** -- Take shuttle boat across lake (15 min), then easy 1-mile round trip walk to Hidden Falls.
- **Oxbow Bend** -- Iconic Teton reflection photo spot, excellent wildlife viewing (moose, eagles, otters).
- **Mormon Row** -- Historic barns with Grand Teton backdrop. Easy walking, great photos.

**Day 8: Jackson Hole Town Day (Rest Day)**
- Explore Jackson Town Square (elk antler arches)
- Jackson Hole Rodeo (summer evenings, very family-friendly)
- National Museum of Wildlife Art
- Shopping and browsing downtown galleries
- Snake River Brewery (family-friendly with kids menu, outdoor play area)
- Lunch at one of Jackson's restaurants

**Day 9: Flexible / Repeat Favorites**
- Revisit favorite spots
- Boiling River hot spring (easy 5-10 min walk, natural hot spring swimming -- check if suitable for babies)
- Norris Geyser Basin -- Boardwalk through the hottest and most dynamic thermal area
- Mud Volcano area -- Short boardwalk, dramatic bubbling features

**Day 10: Departure Day**
- Pack up, final morning in West Yellowstone
- Drive back to Bozeman airport (1h 45m)

### Horseback Riding
- Available at Jackson Lake Lodge and Colter Bay Village in Grand Teton
- 1-hour and 2-hour guided rides
- **Minimum age: 8 years old, minimum height: 4 feet**
- Not suitable for the 5-year-old or babies
- Some adults could ride while others watch the children
- Cost: ~$55-$85/person for 1 hour

### Boat Activities
- **Jenny Lake Scenic Cruise:** Adults $30, Seniors $27, Children 2-11 $25
- **Snake River Float Trip** (Grand Teton): Family-friendly calm-water floats, ~$75-$95/adult, ~$55-$65/child
- Great for wildlife viewing from the water (moose, eagles, beavers)

### Activity Costs
- Jenny Lake Cruise (12 people): ~$300
- Snake River Float (optional, adults only or older child): ~$400-$600
- Horseback riding (optional, select adults): ~$200-$400
- **Total activities estimate: $500-$1,300**

## 7. PRACTICAL TIPS

### Cell Service
- **Extremely limited inside Yellowstone.** Verizon has the best coverage but still spotty.
- Some signal at Old Faithful area, Mammoth Hot Springs, Canyon Village
- No reliable signal in Lamar Valley, most backcountry areas, and between major junctions
- West Yellowstone and Gardiner have good cell service
- **Must-do:** Download offline Google Maps for the entire park area. Carry a paper park map (free at entrance stations).

### Weather in August
- **Daytime highs:** 70-85F (21-29C)
- **Nighttime lows:** 35-45F (2-7C) -- it gets genuinely cold at night
- **Afternoon thunderstorms** are common and arrive quickly
- **Elevation:** Park sits at 6,000-8,800 feet; the sun is intense
- UV index is very high at elevation; sunburn happens fast

### What to Pack
- **Layers:** T-shirts, fleece/light jacket, warm jacket for mornings and evenings
- **Rain gear:** Waterproof jackets for everyone (afternoon storms)
- **Sun protection:** High SPF sunscreen, SPF lip balm, wide-brimmed hats, sunglasses
- **Footwear:** Sturdy hiking shoes or good athletic shoes (boardwalks can be uneven)
- **Baby gear:** Sun shades for car seats, warm layers for babies (mornings are cold), baby carrier (front-carry and/or backpack carrier for the 5-year-old if they tire)
- **Insect repellent:** Mosquitoes and biting flies in August
- **Binoculars:** Essential for wildlife viewing at safe distances
- **Cooler:** For packing daily lunches and snacks
- **Reusable water bottles:** Stay hydrated at altitude
- **First aid kit:** Include children's medications, band-aids, antibiotic ointment

### Wildlife Safety with Kids
- **NEVER approach wildlife.** Stay at least 100 yards (91m) from bears and wolves, 25 yards (23m) from all other animals including bison and elk.
- Bison injure more visitors than any other animal. They look slow but can run 35 mph.
- Keep children within arm's reach at all times near thermal features and wildlife.
- Carry bear spray ($30-$50, available for purchase or rental in West Yellowstone).
- **STAY ON BOARDWALKS.** The ground near thermal features is thin crust over boiling water. This is the #1 safety rule with kids.
- Store food in vehicles (not tents) to avoid attracting bears.
- Never run from a bear -- back away slowly and make yourself appear large.

### Altitude Considerations
- Yellowstone elevation: 6,000-8,800 feet (most attractions around 7,500-8,000 ft)
- **Babies under 1:** Infants, especially those under 3 months, are more sensitive to low oxygen at altitude. Your babies are under 1, so monitor them for excessive fussiness, poor feeding, or unusual sleepiness. The risk decreases after 6 months.
- **5-year-old:** Children tolerate altitude similarly to adults. Watch for headaches, nausea, loss of appetite.
- **Everyone:** Drink extra water, eat carb-rich foods, take it easy on the first day, avoid alcohol on arrival day.
- **Coming from sea level (SFO):** You will notice the altitude. Shortness of breath on exertion is normal. Symptoms usually resolve within 24-48 hours.
- Consult your pediatrician before the trip regarding the babies and altitude.

## 9. NEARBY ATTRACTIONS

### Grand Teton National Park
- Located immediately south of Yellowstone (connected by John D. Rockefeller Jr. Parkway)
- **Free entry if you have America the Beautiful pass**
- Worth at least 1 full day, ideally 2
- Highlights:
  - **Jenny Lake:** Scenic cruises, shuttle boat to Hidden Falls, easy lakeside walks
  - **Oxbow Bend:** Stunning Teton reflections, moose and eagle sightings
  - **Snake River Overlook:** Famous Ansel Adams photo spot
  - **Mormon Row Historic District:** Iconic barns with Teton backdrop
  - **Signal Mountain Summit:** 5-mile drive to panoramic viewpoint (no hiking required)
  - **Colter Bay Visitor Center:** Excellent Native American art collection
  - **Snake River float trips:** Calm scenic floats, family-friendly

### Jackson Hole, Wyoming
- Upscale mountain resort town, about 1.5-2 hours from West Yellowstone
- **Town Square:** Famous elk antler arches at all four corners
- **Jackson Hole Rodeo:** Wednesday and Saturday nights in summer, family-friendly ($20-$40/person)
- **National Museum of Wildlife Art:** 14 galleries, stunning views ($17 adults, kids free under 5)
- **Jackson Hole Aerial Tram:** Ride to 10,450 feet at Jackson Hole Mountain Resort for stunning views ($47/adult, $30/child, under 5 free). Waffles at the summit.
- **Shopping:** Western art galleries, outdoor gear shops, souvenir stores
- **Dining:** Excellent restaurants -- Snake River Brewery (kid-friendly), Persephone Bakery, Bin22

### Other Nearby Points of Interest
- **Quake Lake / Earthquake Lake Visitor Center** (en route from Bozeman to West Yellowstone): Fascinating geology lesson
- **Grizzly & Wolf Discovery Center** (West Yellowstone): Indoor/outdoor exhibits with live bears and wolves. Great for rainy day or for kids to see animals up close. ~$15/adult, $10/child.
- **Yellowstone IMAX Theater** (West Yellowstone): 40-minute film about Yellowstone. Good for a rest break.
- **Museum of the Rockies** (Bozeman): World-class dinosaur exhibit. Great if you have time before/after flights. $14.50/adult.

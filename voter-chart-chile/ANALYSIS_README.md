
CHILE VOTER DEMOGRAPHICS ANALYSIS - INTERPRETATION
==================================================

This analysis reproduces the US "education-income voting gap" chart for Chile,
using presidential election data from 1999 to 2025.

KEY FINDINGS:
-------------

1. OPPOSITE PATTERN FROM THE US:
   - In the US, Democrats moved from the bottom-left (working class) to top-right (educated/wealthy)
   - In Chile, the LEFT was historically in the bottom-left (working class support)
   - The RIGHT was historically in the top-right (educated/wealthy support)

2. TRADITIONAL PATTERN (1999-2017):
   - The Chilean left consistently performed ~15-20pp better among low education voters
   - The Chilean left consistently performed ~17-24pp better among low income voters
   - This matches the traditional "class-based" voting pattern

3. DRAMATIC SHIFT (2021-2025):
   - In 2021 (Boric vs Kast), the education and income gaps nearly disappeared
   - In 2025 (Jara vs Kast), the pattern REVERSED - the left did slightly better among educated voters

4. IMPLICATIONS:
   - Chile is experiencing a "realignment" similar to what happened in the US/Europe
   - The working class is shifting toward the right (Kast, Republicanos)
   - The educated middle class is becoming more receptive to the left
   - This matches the global trend of "cultural" issues displacing "economic" issues

DATA SOURCES:
-------------
- Election results: SERVEL (Servicio Electoral de Chile) - official results by comuna
- Socioeconomic data: CASEN surveys, Census 2017/2024, AIM Chile GSE classification
- Education level: % of population with university degree by comuna
- Income level: GSE (Grupo Socioeconómico) classification and average household income

METHODOLOGY:
------------
1. Classified comunas into high/low education based on % with university degree
   - High education: >= 30% with degree
   - Low education: < 20% with degree

2. Classified comunas into high/low income based on GSE distribution
   - High income: Predominantly ABC1/C2 (high and medium-high GSE)
   - Low income: Predominantly D/E (low GSE)

3. Calculated average left-candidate vote share for each group
4. Computed gaps: High group - Low group
